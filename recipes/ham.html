Honey Glazed Ham

Part-boiled and extra tender

Perfect for a family gathering. Boiling creates a depth of flavour with a sweet honey and mustard glaze finish.

Ingredients

  • Boneless unsmoked gammmon joint, tied, ~2.5kg
  • 1 sprig thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 rosemary spring
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 1 tbsp black peppercorns
  • 1 tsp cloves
  • 2 tbsp English mustard
  • 70g clear honey
  • 2 tbsp soft dark brown sugar

Steps

  1. Place gammon in a deep saucepan, leaving tied. Tie the rosemary, bay leaves, thyme together and add along with the onion, carrts, peppercorns and cloves.
  2. Cover with cold water and bring to the boil, cover with a lid and simmer fo 1 hr 15 mins, checking occasionally to remove scum from the surface.
  3. Remove joint from the liquid and leave to cool. Once cool enough to handle remove the thick skin, leaving the thick layer of fat. Score the fat in a criss-cross pattern.
  4. Pre-heat the oven to 200C (180C fan). Place the ham in a roasting tray lined with foil. Mix the honey, mustard and sugar and brush generously over the ham.
  5. Place in the oven for 15 minutes, remove and brush with more of the mixture, returning for 30 minutes. Remove and leave to cool before carving.
